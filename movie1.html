Chungking Express

Wong Kar Wai

This movie came out in 1994 and is 11 years older than me. I first watched it at 14, as a freshman in high school. I remember falling in love with the soundtrack—Dennis Brown’s “Things in Life” and The Mamas & the Papas’ “California Dreamin’.”

I still listen to these songs often but then I listened to be closer to the movie, closer to HongKong in the 1990s, and closer to the actors.

For me, this movie represented my coming-of-age years because I watched it while I was discovering my interests and shaping my identity. I felt proud to be someone who watched Chungking Express.
